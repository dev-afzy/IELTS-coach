TASK 2 PROMPT: Some people believe that unpaid community service should be a compulsory part of high school programmes (for example, working for a charity, improving the neighbourhood or teaching sports to younger children). To what extent do you agree or disagree?
TASK 2 ANSWER (251 words):
Education is one of the most important things in a young person's life, and schools have a big responsibility to prepare students for the future. In this essay I will discuss how schools can help students to be ready for their adult life.

Firstly, schools should teach practical skills that students can use in the real world. Many students leave school without knowing how to manage money, cook a meal or write a job application. These skills are very useful and schools should include them in the timetable so that young people are not lost when they finish their studies.

Secondly, part-time jobs are a great way for teenagers to gain experience. When a student works in a shop or a restaurant at the weekend, they learn how to be responsible, how to work in a team and how to deal with customers. This experience is often more valuable than what they learn from textbooks.

Moreover, technology should play a bigger role in modern classrooms. Students today are very comfortable with computers and phones, so teachers should use these tools to make lessons more interesting and to prepare students for a workplace that is increasingly digital.

In conclusion, schools should focus on practical skills, encourage part-time work and make good use of modern technology in order to prepare young people for adult life. If schools do these things, students will be much more confident and successful when they eventually leave school and enter the world of work.
