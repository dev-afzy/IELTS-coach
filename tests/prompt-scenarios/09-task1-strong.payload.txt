TASK 1 PROMPT: The pie charts below show the proportion of energy produced from five sources in a country in 2000 and in 2020. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.
TASK 1 VISUAL DATA (for accuracy checking): Two pie charts, percentage of total energy produced. 2000: Coal 45%, Gas 20%, Nuclear 15%, Hydro 12%, Renewables 8%. 2020: Coal 22%, Gas 28%, Nuclear 10%, Hydro 12%, Renewables 28%.
TASK 1 ANSWER (182 words):
The two pie charts compare the sources of energy generation in a particular country in the years 2000 and 2020, each measured as a percentage of the total.

Overall, the most striking change was the country's marked shift away from coal towards gas and renewable energy, while hydroelectric power remained unchanged throughout the two decades.

In 2000, coal was by far the dominant source, accounting for almost half of all energy produced at 45%. Gas supplied a fifth of the total, followed by nuclear at 15% and hydro at 12%, with renewables making up only a marginal 8%.

By 2020, the picture had altered considerably. Coal's share had more than halved to just 22%, while renewables had surged to 28%, drawing level with gas, which had itself risen modestly from 20% to become the joint-largest source. Nuclear, by contrast, edged down from 15% to 10%. Hydroelectricity was the only source to hold perfectly steady, contributing an identical 12% in both years and thereby rising in relative importance as coal declined.
